History of DairyMilk

In 1905, Cadbury launched Dairy Milk in the UK, which quickly became one of the most popular milk chocolate brands, celebrated for its high milk content and creamy texture. Following this, dairy milk chocolate experienced significant global expansion throughout the 20th century, with major brands like Hershey's and Mars developing their own versions, further solidifying its place as a beloved confectionery staple around the world.

Ingredients

Dairy Milk typically consists of milk, sugar, cocoa solids, cocoa butter, and emulsifiers like lecithin. The main ingredient is milk, which provides creaminess and flavor, while sugar adds sweetness. Cocoa solids and cocoa butter contribute to the chocolate flavor and texture. Emulsifiers help ensure a smooth consistency, making Dairy Milk a beloved chocolate choice.
